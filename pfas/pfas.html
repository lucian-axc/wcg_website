Integration of Compact Raman Spectrometer for Innovative PFAS Field Measurements

Who...

It involves a collaboration between WaterCare Guard, Syddansk Universitet and LightNovo ApS, formalized in the Videnbroprojekt application of CLEAN Videnbro Ekspres

Why...

PFAS environmental contaminations have in recent years come to cause great concern to public attention and governmental agencies due to their presence in numerous waterways, great persistence in the environment and ability to accumulate in and degrade living organisms. One major challenge of handling PFAS contaminations is the lack of a quick, accurate and cost-effective method for identifying these compounds on site; standard methods presume specialized laboratories and the time-consuming, costly transport of samples from the sites to them.

How

Our project aims to solve the issue of faster, cheaper detection of PFAS contamination by creating an Internet of Things system which relies on the deployment of a mobile, compact device able to provide real-time measurements directly in the field. This will drastically reduce the time from sampling to viewing results and enable public or private agencies to take decisions much faster and continuously monitor water quality. This compact device utilizes Raman spectroscopy through an embedded miniature Raman spectrometer which communicates with a web application controlling the system and where data can be stored, as well as interpreted by machine learning algorithms to monitor, predict and better analyze the content of the liquid samples.
